## 2. Identification Card Policy
> Source: Section 1.7

- All enrolled students must wear their ID card visibly using a **lanyard/leis** inside the campus **at all times**.
- New ID cards are issued by OUR; SWAFO keeps surrendered, lost, and found ID cards.
- **Persons in authority have the right to confiscate a student's ID card** when a violation is committed.

### What Happens to the ID When Confiscated

| Situation | Action |
|---|---|
| Student violates a policy | ID confiscated by accosting authority; student proceeds to SWAFO for a campus pass |
| Student fails to present ID at entry | COR surrendered to guard; guard issues campus pass; student presents ID to SWAFO the next day to claim COR |
| Student loses ID | Campus pass issued; student presents COR to SWAFO; applies for new ID |
| Student uses damaged ID | Campus pass issued; student applies for a new ID and surrenders damaged ID to SWAFO |
| Student changes/shifts course | Old ID surrendered to SWAFO; new ID applied at OUR upon enrollment |

### Applying for a New ID
1. Proceed to SWAFO and accomplish applicable form (lost/damaged/updated)
2. Secure campus pass (valid for 3 consecutive days to find missing ID before declaring as lost)
3. Present notarized affidavit of loss to SWAFO for a referral form after the 3-day allowance
4. Referral form presented to OUR with payment for new ID processing
5. Secure campus pass from SWAFO until new ID is issued

## 3. Persons in Authority
> Source: Section 1.5

The following are recognized as persons in authority at DLSU-D:
- University administrators
- Faculty members
- SWAFO Director
- Student Formatores
- Security Guards

Support staff (housekeepers) must **report** violations witnessed to the SWAFO Director and student formatores.

All persons in authority exercise **"in loco parentis"** — they have the right and duty to accost and refer any erring student to SWAFO for appropriate action.

## 5. Discipline Procedure (Section 26)
> Source: Section 26.2

### Step-by-Step Process

```
1. COMPLAINT / REPORT FILED
   Anyone may file a complaint/report to SWAFO for misconduct.
   Gender-based harassment → filed to CODI (CHED Memo No. 3 s. 2022)

2. EVALUATION BY HEAD OF INVESTIGATION SECTION
   ├── No justifiable grounds → recommends dismissal to SWAFO Director
   └── Justifiable grounds → case disseminated to investigation section

3. NOTICE TO RESPONDENT STUDENT
   - Written notice stating nature and cause of allegation
   - Student given 3 school days to submit written answer/evidence
   - Failure to respond within prescribed period = waiver of right to defend

4. PRELIMINARY INVESTIGATION
   ├── Student admits allegation → investigator prepares and serves formal charge
   └── Student denies allegation → follow-up investigation conducted

5. FOLLOW-UP INVESTIGATION (if needed)
   - Investigator gathers additional evidence
   - May invite complainant / witnesses for questioning
   ├── Allegation not meritorious → investigation report recommending dismissal
   └── Allegation meritorious → formal charge served to respondent

6. INVESTIGATION REPORT PREPARED
   - Investigator prepares report with findings and recommendations
   - Submitted to SWAFO Director for proper hearing

7. SWAFO DIRECTOR HEARING
   - Director may call assistant director and investigator to discuss
   - Director conducts proper hearing with respondent present
   - Student reserves the right to be assisted by counsel
   - Director renders decision on scheduled time/date

8. DECISION
   - Decision of SWAFO Director is final and executory
   - Student may appeal within 5 school days to University Discipline Board (UDB)

   Grounds for appeal:
   - Grave abuse of discretion by SWAFO Director
   - Absence of due process
```

## 6. University Discipline Board (UDB)
> Source: Section 26.3

### Composition
- OSS Dean (Chair)
- College Dean/Associate Dean of the appellant
- USG President or representative
- POLCA President or representative
- CSG of the college of the appellant

### Appeal Procedure

| Step | Details |
|---|---|
| Filing | Within 5 school days after receipt of SWAFO decision; 5 copies filed to OSS |
| Documentation | True copy kept at SWAFO; SWAFO submits original documents to UDB chair 1 school day after appeal filing |
| Initial Determination | OSS Dean convenes board to check sufficiency of grounds |
| Dismissal | If appeal is without merit or grounds are insufficient, case is dismissed |
| Hearing | If sufficient, hearing schedules are set; appellant and guardian notified |
| Time Limit | Hearing should not last beyond 1 calendar month from initial hearing date |
| Decision | Rendered within 10 school days from last hearing; in writing, stating reasons |
| Motion for Reconsideration | May be filed within 5 school days from receipt of decision, only on ground of newly discovered evidence |
| Distribution | Copies sent to all parties, VCAR, College Deans, OUR, SWAFO, and CHED (if necessary) |

## 7. Formation Program
> Source: Section 26.4 and Appendix D

### Who Must Undergo the Formation Program
- Students who have served/finished their sanction (suspension or alternative sanction)
- Students who committed a major offense, whether currently enrolled, transferred, or graduated

### Purpose
To help students with violations recognize their mistakes and learn from them. Completion is required to secure a **Certificate of Good Moral Character**.

### Key Rules
- Enlist at SWAFO from first day of classes until before first session
- Must submit COR and Certificate of Completion at first meeting
- Must complete all sessions
- Only 1 absence allowed with approved letter of explanation
- A student may only attend and complete the program once
- If a student commits another major offense after the program, their right to a Certificate of Good Moral Character is forfeited

## 8. Section 27 — Code of Conduct: Offense Classification System
> Source: Section 27

Offenses are classified into three types:
1. **Minor Offenses**
2. **Major Offenses** (Misconduct / Dishonesty / Violent Acts)
3. **Traffic-Related Offenses** (Minor and Major)

---

### Minor Offense Escalation Table

| Frequency of Violation | Sanction Imposed |
|---|---|
| 1st | Written warning (with verbal advice) |
| 2nd | First minor offense |
| 3rd | Second minor offense |
| 4th | Third minor offense — **equivalent to a major offense** |

> Key rule: **4 minor offenses of the same nature = treated as a major offense**
> See also: Section 27.3.1.43 — Commission of third minor offense is itself a major offense (Sanction 1 on 1st, 2 on 2nd, 3 on 3rd, 4 on 4th)
> See also: Section 27.3.1.39 — Habitual commission of minor offenses (same nature) is a major offense

---

### Major Offense Sanction Definitions

| Sanction Code | Name | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Probation (1 year) | Student is warned; permitted to continue education as long as misconduct stops |
| 2 | Suspension (3–5 school days) | Denial from attending classes for minimum 3 to maximum 5 school days |
| 3 | Suspension (6–12 school days) | Denial from attending classes for minimum 6 to maximum 12 school days |
| 4 | Non-readmission | Denied enrollment for the school term immediately following the term of the decision |
| 5 | Exclusion | Name dropped from the roll of students immediately upon resolution |
| 6* | Expulsion | Student declared disqualified for admission to any public or private HEI in the Philippines |
| Alternative Sanction | Optional | Given at discretion of SWAFO Director in lieu of suspension; details discussed during promulgation |

> *Expulsion is cited from the Manual of Regulations for Private Higher Education (MORPHE) 2008.
> Note: Maximum suspension days should be observed if student opts for alternative sanction.
> If student is a graduation candidate or transferee but has not served sanction, they must serve equivalent penalty before clearance is issued.
> All students who commit major offenses must undergo the Formation Program.

## 15. Student Clearance
> Source: Section 14

Submission of a completed **Student Clearance Form** is a strict requirement for all enrolled and non-enrolled students before securing any official school record from OUR.

The accomplished Student Clearance Form serves as proof that the requesting student is:
- Cleared of all **administrative accountabilities**
- Cleared of all **financial obligations** to the University

> Development note: Clearance is tied to violation status. A student with an unserved sanction cannot be issued clearance or graduate without first serving the equivalent penalty.

### Duplicate Case Detection Rules

- Check student record for same violation code within current academic year
- Flag if same Section 27 code appears more than once for the same student
- Auto-escalate sanction level based on frequency per offense code
- Flag 27.3.1.39 trigger: 3+ minor offenses of the same nature = habitual offense (major)
- Flag 27.3.1.43 trigger: If a student's minor offense count hits 3, the next filing = Section 27.3.1.43
